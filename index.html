Common Concerns
Buying property abroad feels risky.
Let's address that first.

These are the exact questions every international buyer asks us. Here's the honest answer to each.

QCan foreigners actually buy property in Japan?
Yes. Japan places no restrictions on foreign property ownership. You can buy, own, and sell real estate exactly as a Japanese citizen can.
QDo I need a visa to purchase a home?
No visa is required to own property. Many of our clients purchase as an investment or second home without ever relocating to Japan.
QWhat if I don't speak Japanese?
Every step — from property search to contract signing — is handled in English by our bilingual team. Legal documents are translated and explained clearly.
QCan I complete the purchase without visiting Japan?
Yes. We support remote closing via power of attorney, video viewings, and digital document signing for clients who cannot travel.

FAQ
A few more things you might be wondering.
What are the ongoing costs of owning property in Japan?
Annual property tax, and if applicable, management fees for condominiums. We provide a full cost breakdown before you commit to any property.
Can I rent out my property while I'm not using it?
Yes. We offer rental and property management support, including short-term rental (Airbnb-style) setups where permitted by local regulations.
How long does the entire process typically take?
From initial consultation to closing, most purchases complete within 4–8 weeks, depending on financing and property type.
Do you only work with buyers, or also help after purchase?
Our relationship continues after closing — renovation coordination, rental management, and ongoing support are all part of our service.
